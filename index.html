PHYS366: Special Topics in Astrophysics: Statistical Methods

Course Description

This course is intended to provide an introduction to modern statistical methodology and its applications to problems in astrophysics and cosmology. The course is aimed at graduate students intending to do research in astrophysics and cosmology, and we strongly encourage most first and second year students working in KIPAC to take the course. Our goal is to provide a background that will be directly relevant to the kind of problems that typical KIPAC students will encounter in their research.

Course Objectives

Our goal is that students taking this course will:
  • develop familiarity in working with various types of astronomical data.
  • understand the role of modeling in data analysis.
  • develop facility with various types of inference from data.
  • be able to critically evaluate and apply commonly used statistical methodologies.
  • be able to apply advanced statistical reasoning to problems they are likely to encounter in their research.
